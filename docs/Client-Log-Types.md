# Psychologist Patient Logging Protocols

## 1. Mood Diary
**Target Condition:** Depression, Bipolar Disorder, Emotional Regulation
**Purpose:** To track emotional fluctuation and identify external triggers over time.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `Time of Day`: (Morning, Afternoon, Evening)
    * `Current Emotion`: (e.g., Sad, Anxious, Irritable, Content)
    * `Trigger/Context`: Who were you with? What were you doing?
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Mood Intensity`: Scale 1–10 (1 = Mild, 10 = Extreme)
    * `Energy Level`: Scale 1–10 (1 = Lethargic, 10 = Manic/Hyper)

---

## 2. CBT Thought Record
**Target Condition:** Anxiety, Depression, Cognitive Distortions
**Purpose:** To identify "automatic negative thoughts" and restructure them.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `The Situation`: Factually, what happened?
    * `Automatic Thought`: What went through your mind?
    * `Cognitive Distortion`: (e.g., Catastrophizing, All-or-Nothing thinking)
    * `Alternative Thought`: A more balanced perspective.
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Belief in Automatic Thought`: 0–100%
    * `Emotion Intensity`: 0–100%
    * `Belief in Alternative Thought`: 0–100% (Post-exercise)

---

## 3. Sleep Log (CBT-I)
**Target Condition:** Insomnia, Sleep Apnea, Circadian Rhythm Disorders
**Purpose:** To track sleep efficiency and hygiene.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `Bedtime`: Time got into bed.
    * `Lights Out`: Time attempted to sleep.
    * `Wake Time`: Time woke up for the day.
    * `Substance Use`: Caffeine, Alcohol, Heavy meals (Time/Amount).
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Sleep Onset Latency`: Minutes (Time taken to fall asleep)
    * `Number of Awakenings`: Count
    * `Sleep Quality`: Scale 1–5 (1 = Very Poor, 5 = Very Good)
    * `Restedness`: Scale 1–10 (1 = Exhausted, 10 = Fully Refreshed)

---

## 4. Panic & Anxiety Log
**Target Condition:** Panic Disorder, GAD, Agoraphobia
**Purpose:** To objective panic symptoms and duration.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `Trigger`: Context prior to onset.
    * `Physical Symptoms`: (e.g., Racing heart, sweating, dizziness).
    * `Catastrophic Thoughts`: (e.g., "I am dying," "I am going crazy").
    * `Behavioral Response`: (e.g., Escaped situation, controlled breathing).
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Peak Distress`: Scale 0–10
    * `Duration`: Minutes (actual duration of the attack)

---

## 5. Behavioral Activation Log
**Target Condition:** Depression (Major Depressive Disorder)
**Purpose:** To link specific activities to changes in mood.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `Time Block`: (e.g., 9:00 AM – 10:00 AM)
    * `Activity`: Specific description of action taken.
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Mastery`: Scale 0–10 (Sense of accomplishment)
    * `Pleasure`: Scale 0–10 (Sense of enjoyment)

---

## 6. Exposure Hierarchy (ERP)
**Target Condition:** OCD, Phobias, PTSD
**Purpose:** To track habituation to feared stimuli.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `Exposure Task`: The specific fear being faced.
    * `Predicted Outcome`: What the patient thinks will happen.
    * `Actual Outcome`: What actually happened.
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Pre-Exposure SUDs`: Scale 0–100 (Subjective Units of Distress)
    * `Peak SUDs`: Scale 0–100 (Highest distress during task)
    * `Post-Exposure SUDs`: Scale 0–100 (Distress 20 mins after task)

---

## 7. Food & Emotional Eating Log
**Target Condition:** Binge Eating Disorder, Bulimia, Emotional Eating
**Purpose:** To identify emotional triggers for food intake.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `Food/Drink Consumed`: Item and quantity.
    * `Context`: Location and Company.
    * `Compensatory Behavior`: (e.g., Purging, excessive exercise).
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Hunger Level`: Scale 1–10 (Physical hunger before eating)
    * `Fullness Level`: Scale 1–10 (Physical fullness after eating)
    * `Emotional State`: Categorical (Bored, Sad, Anxious, Lonely)

---

## 8. Chronic Pain Log
**Target Condition:** Chronic Pain, Somatic Symptom Disorder
**Purpose:** To decouple physical sensation from emotional suffering.
* **Input Fields:**
    * `Activity`: Action performed during flare-up.
    * `Time of Day`: When the flare-up occurred.
* **Qualifiers & Scales:**
    * `Pain Intensity`: Scale 0–10
    * `Interference`: Scale 0–10 (How much it stopped daily functioning)
    * `Emotional Distress`: Scale 0–10 (Anger/Sadness regarding the pain)
